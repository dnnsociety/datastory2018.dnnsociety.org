Submission Guidelines

Preparation of work

Once the participants form their group, they can start to work on the project at their own paces. All the works should be relevant to the theme stipulated in the topic (will be announced during the briefing).


Originality

The story should be participants’ original work.

Works derived from term papers or assignments previous classes or previous projects or other on-going projects are permitted, only if the participants can clearly indicate how the present work is substantively different from the previous work, and how the present work advances new knowledge or new insights of the topic.

If you are not sure whether the submission is feasible, a rule of thumb is to upload the previous work as an Appendix for the Reviewer Panel.


Submission

Each group can choose to submit ONE of the followings:

    For written story

    • Files for submit : one single PDF file
    • Language :  Chinese or English
    • Length :  2000-4000 words
    • Figures and Tables are encouraged. But the total number of Figures plus Tables should be restricted to less than 5.
      Here “Figure” means all types of static graphical illustrations
      (i.e., such as conventional bar charts, pie charts, matrix, maps, flow charts, etc).

    For video story

    • Files for submit : one video  +  one script
      It could be a short documentary, or a video presentation taping an oral presentation, or an animation illustrating the work flow, visualization tool, and major results.
    • Language :  Mandarin, Cantonese, or English
    • Length :  within 120 seconds
    • Formats :  Quicktime, MPEG, AVI (Video for Windows), or WMV (Windows Media Video)

    For web-based story

    • Files for submit : the URL  +  a folder containing all the web pages and codes.
    • Language :  Chinese or English
    • Length :  2000-4000 words
    • Figures and Tables are encouraged. But the total number of Figures plus Tables should be restricted to less than 5.
      Here “Figure” means all types of static or interactive illustrations.

Supplymentary files

Three supplementary files are mandatory, and should be submitted with the work.

  1. A document illustrating the technical details. (i.e., what kinds of technics are used during the production)
  2. A document reporting the access methods to the original data file used for the story.
    If the data set is from a third-party source or public data (i.e., governmental statistical report), the participants are required to provide the full URL or other pointer for the reviewers to obtain the data.
  3. All the groups should submit one form on the labor distribution among the group members.
  4. Any other supplementary documents (optional)
